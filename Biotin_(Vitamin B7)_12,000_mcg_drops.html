Biotin (Vitamin B7) 12000 mcg – Information Summary

Product Overview

  • Biotin (Vitamin B7): 12,000 mcg (12 mg) per dose
  • Form: Liquid drops, often in an MCT oil, glycerin, or purified water base
  • Purpose: Hair and nail growth, skin health, metabolism of fats, carbs, and amino acids

How to Take

  • Typically 1 ml (or dropperful) daily, under the tongue or mixed with water
  • Take in the morning with food for best effect
  • Shake well before use if suspended in oil or glycerin

Why Biotin Matters

  • Essential for keratin production – supporting strong hair, skin, and nails
  • Involved in glucose regulation and energy metabolism
  • Supports adrenal, thyroid, and nervous system health

Expected Benefits

  • Thicker, faster-growing hair and nails (results in 3–8 weeks)
  • Reduction in hair thinning or brittle nails
  • Improved energy and clearer skin in some users

Safety & Dosage Notes

  • Biotin is water-soluble – excess amounts are excreted in urine
  • Doses up to 10,000–15,000 mcg are commonly used for hair health and are considered safe
  • May affect blood test results (especially thyroid or troponin levels) – inform your doctor before lab work

Who Might Benefit

  • Anyone experiencing hair shedding or weak nails
  • People with thyroid imbalances or skin conditions like seborrheic dermatitis
  • Vegans and vegetarians (biotin may be lower in plant-based diets)

Tips for Best Use

Tip Why It Helps
Take in the morning with food Supports metabolism and reduces any nausea
Use daily for at least 2 months Hair/nail cycles take 6–12 weeks to show results
Let your doctor know if you're having blood tests High biotin levels can interfere with lab results
Combine with collagen, zinc, and B-complex Enhances structural and metabolic benefits
This product is generally safe for daily use. Speak to your healthcare provider before use if pregnant, breastfeeding, or undergoing lab tests.
