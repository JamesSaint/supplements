Vitamin C 1000mg + Zinc 20mg – Information Summary

Product Overview

  • Vitamin C (ascorbic acid): 1000 mg per dose
  • Zinc (typically zinc citrate or gluconate): 20 mg per dose
  • Form: Usually tablets, capsules, or effervescent drinks
  • Purpose: Immune support, antioxidant defence, skin and wound healing, energy metabolism

How to Take

  • Take once daily with a meal
  • Best absorbed when taken with food containing fat and protein
  • Can be split into morning and afternoon doses if bowel sensitivity occurs

Why This Combo Works

  • Vitamin C: Enhances white blood cell function, reduces inflammation, boosts collagen synthesis
  • Zinc: Supports immune response, enzyme production, and cellular repair
  • Synergy: Combined use shown to reduce duration and severity of colds, enhance antiviral immunity

Expected Benefits

  • Improved resistance to colds and infections
  • Faster wound healing and skin repair
  • Antioxidant protection for cells and tissues
  • Support for stress recovery and adrenal function

Safety & Dosage Notes

  • Vitamin C: Doses above 1000mg may cause mild diarrhea in some (bowel tolerance varies)
  • Zinc: Long-term use of 40mg+ daily can reduce copper absorption – 20mg is safe for daily use
  • Avoid taking zinc on an empty stomach – may cause nausea

Who Might Benefit

  • Frequent travellers or those exposed to infections
  • People under physical or mental stress
  • Vegans/vegetarians (often low in zinc)
  • Those with poor wound healing, acne, or chronic fatigue

Tips for Best Use

Tip Why It Helps
Take with food Improves zinc absorption and avoids stomach irritation
Stay hydrated if taking 1000mg+ Vitamin C Reduces risk of oxalate buildup and kidney strain
Choose buffered Vitamin C if you have a sensitive stomach Gentler on the gut lining
Cycle zinc occasionally (e.g. 5 days on, 2 days off) Maintains balance with other minerals like copper
This is general guidance. If you're managing a medical condition, pregnant, or on medication, consult a healthcare provider before supplementing.
