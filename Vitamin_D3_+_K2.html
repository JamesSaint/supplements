Vitamin D3 + K2 (4000 IU + 100 mcg MK-7) – Information Summary

Product Overview

  • Vitamin D3 (cholecalciferol): 4000 IU (100 mcg)
  • Vitamin K2 (menaquinone-7, MK-7): 100 mcg
  • Form: Typically softgel or liquid drops in oil base (e.g., MCT or olive oil) for maximum absorption
  • Purpose: Bone strength, calcium regulation, cardiovascular and immune support

How to Take

  • Take once daily, preferably with your main meal for best absorption
  • Fat-soluble—take with healthy fats (e.g., avocado, olive oil, eggs)
  • May be taken in the morning to align with natural Vitamin D rhythms

Why D3 & K2 Work Together

  • D3 increases calcium absorption from the gut
  • K2 directs calcium to the bones and teeth, preventing arterial buildup
  • This synergy supports bone health + vascular protection

Expected Benefits

  • Supports bone density and mineralisation
  • Reduces risk of arterial calcification when calcium supplements are used
  • Enhances immune resilience and mood stability, especially in winter

Safety & Dosage Notes

  • 4000 IU of D3 is the safe upper daily limit for long-term use in adults (EFSA, NIH)
  • 100 mcg K2 MK-7 is effective at activating osteocalcin and matrix GLA-protein (MGP)
  • Safe for most adults, unless taking anticoagulants like warfarin—K2 can interfere with their action

Who Might Benefit

  • People with low sun exposure or darker skin (low natural D synthesis)
  • Adults over 40 (bone density decline, arterial stiffness)
  • Those taking calcium supplements or magnesium
  • Anyone with low Vitamin D levels on blood tests

Tips for Best Use

Tip Why It Matters
Take with fat-containing meals Boosts absorption of fat-soluble D3 & K2
Avoid taking with calcium at the same time Prevents unnecessary calcium spikes
Monitor Vitamin D levels (blood test every 6–12 months) Ensures safe and effective dosing
Consult your doctor if on blood thinners K2 may reduce effectiveness of medications like warfarin
This information is for general guidance only. Always consult a healthcare professional before starting new supplements—especially if you are pregnant, breastfeeding, taking medications, or managing chronic conditions.
